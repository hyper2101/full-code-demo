# Mewtations: Dogma
# Legacy Stacklands DNA Removal Checklist

> Purpose:
> Track leftover mechanics, systems, terminology, and gameplay assumptions inherited from Stacklands-style design that no longer fit the current direction of Mewtations: Dogma.
>
> This file exists to help gradually identify, isolate, refactor, or remove systems that conflict with:
>
> - individuality
> - ideological survival
> - political territory pressure
> - scarcity economy
> - emotional instability
> - tactical conflict
> - grounded dark-comedy tone
>
> IMPORTANT:
> Not every item must be deleted immediately.
> Some systems may survive as temporary scaffolding.
> The goal is to prevent future architectural contamination.

---

# 1. LAND / BIOME CARD DNA

## Legacy Concepts
- Forest Land
- Swamp Land
- Snow Land
- Curse Land
- Volcano Land
- Demon Land
- Infinite biome expansion

## Why It No Longer Fits
Old design:
```text
Biome = resource production tile
````

Current design:

```text
Territory = political/social/economic space
```

The game is no longer about:

* farming infinite biome cards
* expanding a cozy production board

The game IS about:

* trespassing
* territorial control
* extraction rights
* dangerous ruins
* social authority
* unstable regions

## Preferred Replacement

* Districts
* Controlled Zones
* Extraction Areas
* Ruins
* Patrol Territories
* Free Lands
* Forbidden Areas
* Black Market Regions

## Removal Priority

HIGH

# 18. FINAL DESIGN CHECK RULE

Before adding ANY new feature, ask:

```text
Does this reinforce:
- individuality
- instability
- scarcity
- political pressure
- emotional conflict
- territorial survival

OR

Does this accidentally recreate:
- cozy automation
- infinite optimization
- worker efficiency gameplay
- fantasy biome farming
- passive production loops
```

If the answer is the second category:
STOP and reassess before implementation.
